# 03. Malaysian Standards & Compliance

*Construction Professional Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

I submitted a final account on a Malaysia project. The client rejected it.

"This doesn't comply with CIDB standards," they said.

I didn't know what CIDB was.

Turns out: I'd been working wrong the whole time. Wrong format. Wrong documentation. Wrong processes.

I spent weeks redoing everything to CIDB specification.

That's when I learned: **Malaysia has specific standards. Ignore them at your cost.**

## CIDB (Construction Industry Development Board)

**What it is:** Government body that sets construction standards in Malaysia

**What it regulates:**
- Contractor registration & classification
- Project reporting requirements
- Safety standards
- Quality specifications
- Cost documentation formats

**Why it matters:**
- All government projects must comply
- Many private projects follow CIDB standards
- Non-compliance = rejected payments
- Contractor licensing depends on CIDB registration

## SIRIM (Standards & Industrial Research Institute)

**What it is:** National standards body for product & process standards

**What it regulates:**
- Material specifications (concrete, steel, cement)
- Product certification
- Quality assurance processes
- Environmental compliance

**Why it matters:**
- Materials must be SIRIM-certified
- Concrete mix designs must follow SIRIM specs
- Inspectors check SIRIM compliance
- Non-compliance = project halt

## Real Example: CIDB Final Account Format

CIDB requires:
- Itemized costs (not lump sum)
- Evidence for each claim (invoices, timesheets, receipts)
- Comparison to contract BOQ
- Variations documented with approval
- Tax implications shown
- Timeline of payments shown

Miss any of these? Your claim gets rejected.

## What You Need to Know

**For Malaysia projects:**

1. **Check CIDB requirements before starting**
2. **Format all documents to CIDB standard** from day one
3. **Keep SIRIM certifications** for all materials
4. **Document everything** in CIDB-approved format
5. **Submit progress reports** to CIDB on schedule

Non-compliance costs you time and money.

Compliance saves you both.
