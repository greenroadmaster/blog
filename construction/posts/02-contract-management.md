# 02. Contract Management: FIDIC & PAM

*Construction Professional Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

My first project used FIDIC conditions. I didn't understand them.

The contractor said: "This variation is covered under Clause 13.2."

I said: "No it's not."

We fought. For weeks. Lost money. Lost time.

Later, I read FIDIC. He was right.

I just didn't understand the contract I'd signed.

That's when I learned: **contracts aren't optional. They're the law between you and the contractor.**

And FIDIC & PAM are two completely different laws.

## FIDIC (International Standard)

Used for: Large international EPC projects, cross-border work

**Key principle:** Engineer is boss
- Engineer certifies progress (IPC)
- Engineer approves variations
- Engineer decides disputes
- Engineer has quasi-judicial power

**Your role as QS:** Support the Engineer
- Verify contractor's costs
- Advise on fairness
- Calculate financial impact
- Prepare documents

**Speed:** Slower (more documentation, more engineering review)

## PAM (Malaysian Standard)

Used for: Malaysia-based projects, domestic contracts

**Key principle:** Architect is boss, but QS is more independent
- QS certifies progress independently
- Architect approves, but QS's recommendation carries weight
- Disputes go to arbitration directly (no engineer mediation)
- QS's professional judgment matters more

**Your role as QS:** Lead, with Architect support
- You evaluate costs independently
- You advise Architect
- You negotiate with Contractor
- Your certificate is binding

**Speed:** Faster (less bureaucracy, more QS autonomy)

## Real Example: Same Variation, Different Outcome

**Scenario:** Contractor claims RM 500K for design change

**Under FIDIC:**
- Contractor → Engineer
- Engineer asks QS to evaluate
- QS says: "RM 400K is fair"
- Engineer decides: "RM 400K approved"
- Contractor may appeal to arbitration

**Under PAM:**
- Contractor → QS
- QS evaluates: "RM 400K is fair"
- QS advises Architect: "Approve RM 400K"
- Architect approves
- Faster, clearer, QS's role is stronger

## What This Means

**Choose your contract carefully.**

FIDIC = slower, more formal, engineer-driven  
PAM = faster, more direct, QS-driven

If you're good at QS work, PAM gives you more power.

If you want protection through hierarchy, FIDIC is clearer.

Know the contract. Know your role. Know your power.
