# 06. Variation Orders & Claims

*Construction Professional Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

Contractor claimed RM 2 million in variations.

I said: "No. That's not in the contract."

They said: "You told us to do it."

I said: "I told you about the change, but I didn't authorize a RM 2M cost."

We fought. Ended up in arbitration. Cost RM 500K in legal fees.

Arbitrator ruled against me. They got RM 1.8M.

I learned: **If you authorize work without documenting cost impact, you lose.**

## What a Variation Order Is

**VO = written change to scope + approved cost impact**

**Three parts:**
1. **What changed** (design change, site condition, client request)
2. **How it affects the work** (more material, more labor, more time)
3. **What it costs** (RM X for these specific items)

**Without all three: it's not a valid VO.**

## How to Process a VO Correctly

**Step 1: Document the change**
- When did the change occur?
- Who approved it? (in writing)
- Why did it happen?
- What's different from original scope?

**Step 2: Assess impact**
- What additional work is needed?
- How much material? How much labor? How much time?
- Will it delay other activities?
- Cost: use BOQ rates + 10-15% markup

**Step 3: Get approval before work**
- Show contractor the VO assessment
- Get written agreement
- Don't do work "subject to approval"
- If they disagree on cost, negotiate before work starts

**Step 4: Document the work**
- Timesheets of extra labor
- Invoices for extra material
- Site photographs
- Witness statements
- Keep everything

## Real Example: Authorized vs. Unauthorized

**Scenario: Design changes mid-project**

**Wrong way:**
- Architect says: "We need to change the foundation design"
- Contractor starts work
- Later, contractor claims RM 500K
- You: "I didn't approve that cost!"
- Contractor: "You told me to do it. I did it. I want paid."
- You lose

**Right way:**
- Architect says: "We need to change the foundation design"
- You prepare VO: "Additional RM 500K for [specific items]"
- You get written approval (contractor + architect + client)
- Contractor does work
- You pay RM 500K + original contract price
- Everyone's happy

## The Golden Rule

**No work without authorized VO. No VO without documented cost.**

If contractor wants to do work not in contract: **Get VO approval first.**

If you want contractor to change scope: **Prepare VO, get approval, then authorize.**

Protect yourself. Document everything.
