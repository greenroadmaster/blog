# 01. QS Profession & RICS Pathway

*Construction Professional Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

Twenty years ago, I didn't know what a QS was.

I was a site manager. My job: finish projects on time, under budget, safe.

But I was always fighting with the contractor over costs. They'd claim extra money. I'd say no. We'd argue for weeks.

One day, I realized: **I didn't have a methodology. They did.**

The contractor had cost evidence, market rates, price trends. I had... intuition.

That's when I understood: QS isn't just about cost. It's about **methodology. Evidence. Professionalism.**

I decided to get RICS certified.

## Why RICS Matters

RICS (Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors) sets standards for QS work worldwide.

It means:
- **You follow procedures** (not guessing)
- **You have accountability** (your numbers can be challenged)
- **You're part of a profession** (not just "the cost person")
- **You have credibility** (RICS-qualified = trusted)

A RICS-qualified QS walks into a meeting and people listen. Not because of personality. Because of **credentials**.

## The Pathway

RICS APC (Assessment of Professional Competence) isn't a test you pass.

It's a **portfolio you build**.

You demonstrate:
- Cost Estimation & Tendering
- Cost Planning & Budgeting
- Financial Management & Commercial
- Contract Administration
- Change & Final Accounts

You don't memorize these. You **live** them.

That's why the portfolio is your project experience.

## What This Means for You

If you're a construction professional who wants credibility:

**Step 1:** Work on real projects (you probably are)
**Step 2:** Document your work (BOQs, IPCs, VOs, final accounts)
**Step 3:** Write about what you learned
**Step 4:** Submit for RICS assessment

It takes time. It takes discipline.

But when you're certified, you're not "the guy who estimates costs."

You're "the RICS-qualified QS."

That changes how people treat you. And how much you can charge.
