# 08. Project Scheduling Tools

*Construction Professional Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

I managed a project without proper scheduling tool. Just my head and Excel spreadsheet.

Project got delayed. I blamed contractor. Contractor blamed design delays. Designer blamed the client.

Everyone blamed everyone.

When I got audited, I couldn't prove who caused the delay.

Turns out: **without a scheduling tool, you can't prove anything.**

## What Primavera P6 Does

P6 = professional project scheduling software

It tracks:
- Every task
- Dependencies (task A must finish before B starts)
- Resources (who's working on what)
- Progress (planned vs. actual)
- Critical path (which tasks affect the end date)
- Schedule variance (are we ahead or behind?)

## Why This Matters

**During project:**
- You see delays early
- You can adjust schedule
- You know who's behind
- You can accelerate critical path

**After project (if there's a dispute):**
- You have evidence of what happened
- You can show who caused delays
- You can claim time-related costs
- You have proof for arbitration

**No schedule = no proof.**

## Real Example: Proving Responsibility for Delay

**Scenario: Project delayed 2 months. Contractor claims design changes caused it. Designer claims site conditions caused it.**

**Without schedule:**
- Everyone argues
- No one has proof
- Arbitrator guesses
- You lose

**With P6 schedule:**
- You show: "Design change was issued Week 3"
- You show: "Activity X, which depended on design, started Week 5"
- You show: "Activity X was on critical path"
- You show: "Activity X finished 2 weeks late"
- You prove: "Design change caused the delay"
- Arbitrator rules in your favor

**Proof = money.**

## What You Need to Do

**Before project starts:**
- Build detailed schedule in P6
- Identify critical path
- Share with contractor (so they know)

**Every month:**
- Update actual progress
- Track schedule variance
- Report to team

**If dispute arises:**
- You have evidence
- You can show who's responsible

P6 is your insurance policy.
