# 04. Employment & Labor Compliance

*Construction Professional Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

I hired a worker. Didn't put him on formal contract. Paid cash.

When he got injured, he had no workers' comp. Sued me personally.

I lost. Big.

That day I learned: **Malaysian employment law is strict. Ignore it and it destroys you.**

## Malaysian Employment Act Basics

**Key rules:**
- Every employee must have a written contract
- Monthly salary must be paid on time
- Overtime must be compensated
- Leave is mandatory (annual leave, sick leave, public holidays)
- Workers' comp is mandatory (SOCSO)

**Your liability:**
- If you breach contract, employee can sue
- If worker is injured, you're liable (even if it's their fault)
- If you don't pay on time, penalties apply
- If you fire unfairly, employee gets severance + damages

## The Employment Pass Trap

**New rule (June 2026):** Employment Pass tenure is capped

- Foreign workers can work in Malaysia max 6 years on EP
- After 6 years, they must leave for 1 year before returning
- No exceptions
- This affects project planning

**Example:**
- You hire expat in Year 1
- Year 6: they must leave
- If project runs to Year 7, you need replacement
- Replacement takes 2-3 months to arrange
- Your project timeline breaks

**Solution:** Plan for worker rotation in Year 5

## Real Example: Salary Calculation Mistake

You calculate leave deduction wrong. You owe the worker RM 5,000.

- Employee sues
- You lose (law is clear)
- You pay RM 5,000 + legal fees
- Plus penalties

**Correct calculation:**
- Annual leave: 12 days/year (pro-rated if new)
- Daily rate = monthly salary / 26 working days
- Deduction = days not worked × daily rate

Get this right. It's in the Employment Act.

## What You Must Do

**Before hiring anyone:**
1. Prepare written contract (follow Employment Act format)
2. Register with SOCSO (workers' compensation)
3. Ensure EPF contributions (pension)
4. Calculate salary structure correctly
5. Keep records of all payments

**Every month:**
1. Pay on time (not late)
2. Keep attendance records
3. Track leave usage
4. Document any disciplinary action

**Failure = lawsuits + penalties + damages**

Success = peace of mind + loyal workers
