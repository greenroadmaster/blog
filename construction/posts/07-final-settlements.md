# 07. Final Settlements

*Construction Professional Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

Project finished. I thought I was done.

But final account took 6 months.

Contractor claimed RM 2M more. I found RM 1M in overbilling. We negotiated to RM 500K.

By the time we settled, the contractor had moved to another project. Getting them to sign off was like pulling teeth.

I learned: **Final account is the hardest part.**

## What Final Account Is

**Final Account = settling all debts at project end**

It includes:
- All progress payments (already made)
- All variation orders (approved + disputed)
- All time-related costs (extensions, delays)
- All claims (safety, rework, weather)
- Adjustment for price escalation
- Retention release
- Final true-up to actual cost

## The Six-Month Process

**Month 1: Contractor submits**
- Contractor lists everything they claim
- Usually inflated (they start negotiating from high)

**Month 2-3: QS reviews**
- Go through every item
- Check against invoices, timesheets, BOQ
- Identify errors, overbilling, unsupported claims
- Prepare QS assessment

**Month 4: Negotiation**
- Meet with contractor
- Discuss each disputed item
- Negotiate final numbers
- Prepare settlement agreement

**Month 5: Approval**
- Architect reviews QS recommendation
- Client reviews cost impact
- Everyone approves settlement
- Get all signatures

**Month 6: Payment**
- Release final payment
- Get final accounts signed
- Close project

## Real Example: Finding Overbilling

Contractor claims RM 100/m² for concrete work.

BOQ says RM 80/m².

Contractor argues: "Market rates went up. We used more expensive concrete due to availability."

You check:
- Material invoices show RM 75/m² actual cost
- Labor was RM 15/m² (same as BOQ estimate)
- No valid reason for markup

Settlement: RM 90/m² (compromise). You save RM 10/m² × 500m² = RM 5,000

That's your job in final account. Protect costs.

## What You Need to Know

**Final account is where you earn your fee as QS.**

- Review everything
- Challenge unsupported claims
- Keep records
- Don't rush settlement
- Get everything in writing

A good final account saves money and protects relationships.
