# 03. Data Centers: Technology Meets Sustainability

*English Learning Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

I worked on a data centre project in Malaysia. Massive facility. Hundreds of servers running 24/7.

The building was incredible: redundant power systems, cooling towers everywhere, backup generators, fireproofing.

But I remember thinking: **This uses an enormous amount of electricity.**

A single data centre can use as much power as a city of 100,000 people.

And we're building more of them. Everywhere.

That's progress. That's also a problem.

When the OPIc asked about "technology and sustainability," I knew exactly what to talk about.

## The OPIc Question

"Tell me about a technology that's important but has environmental costs. How do we balance progress and environmental protection?"

Data centres. Perfect example.

## How I Answered

"Data centres are crucial for the modern world. Everything—cloud computing, AI, streaming, all of it—requires massive data centres. But they consume enormous amounts of electricity. A single facility can power a city. So we have a dilemma: we need the technology, but the environmental cost is real.

The solution isn't to stop building data centres. It's to build them smarter. Using renewable energy. Better cooling systems. Recycling heat. It's technology solving the problem that technology created."

Notice the structure:
1. **Why it matters** (technology is essential)
2. **The problem** (environmental cost)
3. **The nuance** (not black and white)
4. **The solution** (innovation within the system)

This isn't perfect English. It's clear thinking in English.

## What This Taught Me

**Your professional knowledge is your superpower.**

I don't speak fluent English about abstract topics.

But I speak fluent English about data centres, construction, project management, infrastructure.

Because I understand them.

Use what you know.
