# 07. How Communication Shapes Education

*English Learning Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

I realized something: the way you communicate about something shapes how people understand it.

If I explain construction safety as "follow the rules," people resist.

If I explain it as "I want you to go home to your family tonight," people listen.

Same information. Different communication. Different outcome.

That's the reverse direction: communication shapes education.

How we talk about things determines what people learn.

## The OPIc Question

"Tell me about how communication affects learning or education."

## Real Example: Technical Training

I was training engineers on a new project management system (Primavera P6).

I could have taught it as: "Click here. Then here. Then save."

But I taught it as: "This system helps you track what you promised versus what you delivered. If you promise to finish on time and you're falling behind, this system tells you early. Early warning means you can fix it. Late warning means the client is angry and you're paying penalties."

Different communication. Same software.

The people who understood **why** they were using it learned it faster and used it better.

## The Fluency Connection

**Understanding causation makes you sound intelligent in English.**

Instead of: "I did A, then B, then C happened."

You can say: "Because I did A, understanding that it would lead to B, I achieved C."

That's sophisticated. That's fluent.

## What This Taught Me

**The best educators aren't the ones who know the most. They're the ones who understand how communication works.**
