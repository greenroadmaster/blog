# 10. From Construction Expert to Articulate Speaker

*English Learning Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

I've been in construction for 20+ years. I know my field.

But knowing something and articulating it are different skills.

I knew about infrastructure. But could I explain it to someone who didn't?

I knew about urbanization challenges. But could I discuss them fluently?

That was the real test.

And that's what OPIc AL was about.

Not whether I know English. **Whether I can think and communicate at a high level.**

## The Journey

**Before OPIc:** I could have conversations about daily life. But discussing policy? Infrastructure strategy? Sustainability challenges? I got lost.

**After OPIc:** I can discuss any topic, especially ones I understand from work.

The difference wasn't grammar. It was **confidence in thinking.**

## What Changed

I stopped trying to sound English.

I started trying to be clear.

I stopped worrying about perfect sentences.

I started focusing on clear ideas.

I stopped saying "I'm sorry, my English isn't perfect."

I started saying "Let me explain what I mean."

## The Real Skill

**Articulation is thinking out loud.**

When you can think clearly about complex ideas and express them simply, you're fluent.

You don't need perfect grammar. You need clear thinking.

## What This Means

If you want to speak English at a high level:

1. **Develop expertise** in something you care about
2. **Think deeply** about it in English (not in Korean, not in Malay—in English)
3. **Practice explaining it** to people who don't understand it
4. **Don't apologize** for your English—explain your thinking

## What I Learned

**Language isn't separate from expertise. Language is how you express expertise.**

The better you understand something, the more fluently you can discuss it.

That's not just true for English. It's true for any language.

That's not just true for language. It's true for communication.

That's not just true for communication. It's true for thinking.

Clear thinking = fluent speech.
