# 02. Urbanization & Environment in Johor Bahru

*English Learning Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

Twenty years ago, I first dived off the coast of Johor Bahru. The reef was vibrant. Fish everywhere. Clear water.

I went back five years ago. The same reef was half-dead. Construction debris in the water. Murky visibility.

The city had grown. Rapid development. High-rise buildings. Port expansion.

Progress had a cost.

That's when I understood: **urbanization isn't good or bad. It's complicated.**

And explaining complicated things in English? That's where real fluency shows.

## The OPIc Question

"Tell me about a city you know that's growing rapidly. What are the advantages and disadvantages?"

This wasn't asking for a simple answer. It was asking me to **think.**

Advantages: economic growth, job creation, infrastructure, education opportunities.

Disadvantages: environmental degradation, loss of traditions, inequality, congestion.

But also: **nuance.** The same development that destroys reefs creates hospitals. The same growth that pollutes water provides electricity.

## How to Speak About This Fluently

**Don't memorize. Understand.**

When you understand the complexity, you can explain it naturally:

"Johor Bahru is a good example. The city has grown dramatically in the last 20 years. That's created economic opportunities—lots of jobs, better infrastructure, new schools. But it's also created problems. The coastline is more polluted. The reef I used to dive on is damaged. So it's not simple. Progress always has trade-offs."

Notice: no perfect grammar. Just clear thinking expressed naturally.

## What This Taught Me

**The best English speakers aren't the ones with perfect grammar. They're the ones who think clearly.**

When you understand urbanization—really understand it, from experience—you can talk about it in any language.
