# 04. Rising Cities, Rising Responsibilities

*English Learning Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

Kuala Lumpur wasn't always like this. Thirty years ago, it was a different city.

Now: towers, highways, metro systems, development everywhere.

Same with Johor Bahru. Same with Penang.

Malaysia is urbanizing rapidly.

And with that comes responsibility: to the people living there, to the environment, to the future.

That's the conversation happening now.

And it's happening in English—at conferences, in business meetings, in policy discussions.

If you want to participate, you need to speak about it fluently.

## The OPIc Question

"Tell me about a major city you know. What are the city's main challenges for the future?"

## How I Thought About It

Rising cities have rising challenges:

**Economic:** How do you grow without creating inequality?

**Environmental:** How do you build without destroying nature?

**Social:** How do you manage rapid change without losing culture?

**Infrastructure:** How do you build systems that can handle growth?

These aren't simple. But they're real. And talking about them clearly in English shows genuine fluency.

## The Fluency Principle

**Fluency isn't perfection. It's clarity under complexity.**

A native speaker discussing urban policy doesn't speak perfectly either.

They just:
- Organize their thoughts
- Use connecting words ("however," "on the other hand," "for example")
- Don't apologize for pausing to think
- Explain their reasoning

## What This Taught Me

**Cities aren't just places. They're systems.**

Understanding that helps you speak about them intelligently in any language.
