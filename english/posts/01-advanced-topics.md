# 01. Why I Chose Advanced Topics

*English Learning Journey | June 2026*

## The Story

I sat in the OPIc waiting room, and the proctor asked: "What difficulty level do you want?"

Options: 3-3, 3-4, 4-4.

Everyone around me chose 3-3 or 3-4. Safe. Predictable.

I chose 4-4.

They looked at me like I was crazy.

"Are you sure?" the proctor asked. "4-4 is difficult."

I was sure. Here's why.

## Why Advanced Topics Matter

**At Level 4-4, you don't get easy questions.**

You get asked about urbanization, sustainability, education systems, technology and society.

These are the topics that matter.

And they're the same topics I think about every day—as a construction manager, as someone who understands data centres and infrastructure.

So I chose 4-4 not because I'm brave. **I chose it because speaking about topics I understand is easier than pretending to understand simple ones.**

## The Preparation Strategy

When you choose 4-4, you can't rely on memorized answers.

You need to:
- **Understand the concepts deeply** (not just English words)
- **Think clearly about your own opinions**
- **Connect ideas across different topics**
- **Speak naturally, even when discussing complex ideas**

My preparation wasn't "learn more English vocabulary."

It was "develop your thinking in English."

## What This Taught Me

**Choosing difficulty isn't about proving yourself. It's about honesty.**

If you choose easy topics you don't care about, you sound fake.

If you choose hard topics you understand, you sound like yourself.

The test wasn't evaluating my English. It was evaluating whether I could think and communicate at a high level.

I could. And that's what AL means.
