# Post-Dive Procedures & Decompression Sickness Prevention

*Instructor's Perspective | June 2026*

---

## The Story

A diver: 20m, 45 minutes. Proper ascent. Proper 5m safety stop (3 minutes).

He exited the water. Took off his BCD. Started helping other divers.

12 hours later: sharp pain in shoulder. Arm weakness.

DCS (decompression sickness, "the bends").

He had an undiagnosed PFO (heart hole). Nitrogen bubbles bypassed his lungs instead of off-gassing slowly.

6 hours in a hyperbaric chamber. Permanent partial nerve damage.

Prevention matters.

---

## The Safety Stop: Non-Negotiable

**At 5 meters for minimum 3-5 minutes:**

This is where nitrogen off-gases. If you skip it, nitrogen stays in your bloodstream longer.

**What happens at safety stop:**
- Hover quietly at 5m
- Monitor buddy
- Breathe normally
- Don't move around
- Just wait and let your body do its work

**Ascent Rate:**
- Maximum 10 meters per minute
- If you started at 20m, that's 2 minutes to reach 5m
- Slower is better

**Don't Skip This.**

I've had divers argue: "I wasn't that deep." Doesn't matter. 5m safety stop. Period.

## Post-Dive Protocol

### Exiting the Water

**In order:**
1. Remove fins
2. Remove mask
3. Exit water slowly (no rushing)
4. Sit down on boat immediately
5. Remove BCD only after sitting
6. Rest for 10+ minutes before standing

**Why sit?**
- Prevents blood pooling in legs (can worsen DCS)
- Keeps nitrogen in lungs (where it off-gases)
- Reduces DCS risk dramatically

### Immediate After-Dive (First 10 Minutes)

**Rest:**
- Sit on boat
- No standing
- No physical activity
- No helping other divers yet

**Hydration:**
- Drink fresh water (not salt water, not alcohol)
- Hydration helps nitrogen off-gas faster

**Monitoring:**
- Feel for anything unusual (tingling, pain, weakness)
- Don't dismiss symptoms as "probably nothing"

### Post-Dive Logging

**Log every dive:**
- Date and time
- Site name
- Entry and exit times
- Maximum depth
- Bottom time
- Dive computer data
- Any issues or observations

**Why log?**
- Track your experience progression
- Medical record if DCS develops
- Remember what you saw/learned

### Flying After Diving

**Rule:** Wait 12+ hours before flying or driving to high altitude

**Why?** 
- Cabin pressure at altitude can cause decompression issues
- Your body is still off-gassing nitrogen
- Flying too soon increases DCS risk significantly

**If multiple dives:**
- Wait 18+ hours before flying
- Some agencies say 24 hours for safety

## DCS (Decompression Sickness): Signs & Symptoms

**When it strikes:** Within 30 minutes to 12+ hours post-dive (sometimes up to 24 hours)

### Major Symptoms

**Joint/Muscle Pain ("The Bends"):**
- Usually shoulders, elbows, knees
- Can be severe
- Develops gradually or suddenly
- May start as mild ache, become severe

**Neurological ("The Hits"):**
- Dizziness, loss of balance, vertigo
- Weakness or paralysis (especially legs)
- Tingling, numbness
- Confusion or difficulty concentrating

**Respiratory ("Chokes"):**
- Chest pain, breathing difficulty
- Coughing (dry cough)
- Rare but very serious

**Skin ("Marbling"):**
- Itchy rash, usually on torso
- Blotchy, red skin pattern
- Less common but distinctive

### Less Common Symptoms

- Inner ear DCS (balance problems, hearing loss)
- Spinal cord DCS (paralysis, loss of bowel/bladder control)

## If You Suspect DCS

### Immediate Action

1. **Call DAN (Diver Alert Network):** +1-919-684-9111 (24/7)
2. **Get to nearest hyperbaric chamber immediately** (even if symptoms mild)
3. Breathe 100% oxygen while waiting
4. **Do NOT delay** thinking symptoms will go away

### Why Speed Matters

- Early treatment = full recovery
- Delayed treatment = permanent damage
- Hyperbaric chamber recompresses you (reduces bubble size)
- Oxygen accelerates nitrogen off-gassing

### What Hospital/Chamber Will Do

- Recompress you in pressure chamber
- Increase oxygen levels
- Slowly decompress (mirrors safe dive ascent)
- Usually 4-6 hours per session
- May need multiple sessions

---

## Prevention: The Best Medicine

### Pre-Dive

- Stay well-hydrated (day before)
- Sleep well (night before)
- Don't drink alcohol

### During Dive

- Don't go deeper than planned
- Don't exceed bottom time
- Monitor air consumption
- Stay warm (cold increases DCS risk)

### Ascent

- **Safety stop at 5m minimum 3-5 minutes**
- Ascend slowly (max 10m/minute)
- Don't make rapid ascents

### Post-Dive

- Rest and hydrate
- Don't fly for 12+ hours
- Monitor for symptoms

---

## What I Tell Every Diver

"Do your safety stop. Even if you weren't deep. Even if you weren't long.

The safety stop is where your body finishes its job.

If you feel anything unusual in the next 12 hours—tingling, weakness, pain, dizziness—call DAN immediately. Don't wait. Don't hope it goes away.

Hyperbaric treatment works best when you get it early."
